Register as a Canarian resident!

Step 1: The NIE.

The NIE (Numero Identificacion Extranjeros) is a unique identification number for all foreigners residing in Spain. It is needed if you want to work, open a bank account or buy a house in Spain.
For the application of your NIE, go to your local police station (gobierno) and bring the following documents:

Copy and original passport or ID Copy and original health insurance card Copy and original rent contract NIE Application Form

Step 2: The Residencia.

To finalize the registration, it is necessary to register at your local town hall (ayuntamiento). Often it is necessary to make an appointment in advance. You can do this either online or at one of the machines at the town hall.
On the day of your appointment, don't forget to bring the following documents:

Copy and original passport or ID Copy and original rent contract and proof of last rent payment Your NIE (see Step 1)

If you would like to enjoy the Canarian benefits for entrance and flight fees, you might need a Certificado de Viaje for the first couple of months until you have been registered in the city's system. Just ask for it at your town hall.

Step 3: Enjoying the perks.

Use your status of a Canarian resident to benefit from the promotions on flights, ferries and entrance fees.

  When booking a flight or a ferry, just tick that you are a Canarian resident. The system will check your status based on your name. During the first weeks after your registration, this might not be possible. But don't worry! Just keep your Certificado de Viaje at hand.   For admission fee discounts for museums and sights, just show your NIE card.
